Monkey

Monkeys are highly intelligent and agile primates that belong to the order Primates. They are widely distributed across various regions of the world, including the tropical forests of Africa, Asia, and Central and South America. With over 260 species, monkeys exhibit a vast diversity in size, appearance, and habitat. They are known for their expressive faces, dexterous hands and feet, and complex social structures.

Behavior

Giraffes are social creatures that typically live in loose, open groups called towers. They spend most of their day browsing on leaves, flowers, and fruits, particularly favoring acacia trees. Despite their height, giraffes have a surprisingly graceful gait and can run at speeds up to 35 miles per hour over short distances. Their long necks are not only for feeding but also play a role in male combat, known as "necking," where they swing their necks to strike each other with their heads to establish dominance. Giraffes are generally peaceful animals and use their height and keen vision to keep watch for predators.
