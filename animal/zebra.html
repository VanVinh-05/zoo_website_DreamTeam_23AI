Zebra

Zebras are strikingly beautiful members of the equid family, best known for their distinctive black and white striped coats. These social animals are native to the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. There are three main species of zebras: the plains zebra, the mountain zebra, and the Grevy's zebra, each adapted to different habitats and climates. Zebras are closely related to horses and donkeys but are uniquely adapted to their environments with their bold stripes, which serve various purposes from camouflage to social signaling.

Behavior

Zebras are highly social animals that live in groups called herds or dazzles. These groups are usually made up of one stallion, several mares, and their offspring. Zebras communicate with each other through vocalizations, body language, and their distinctive stripe patterns, which help them recognize each other. They are constantly on the move, grazing on grasses and other vegetation, and they rely on their keen senses of sight, hearing, and smell to detect predators. When threatened, zebras will run in a zigzag pattern to evade capture and will also use their powerful kicks to defend themselves.
